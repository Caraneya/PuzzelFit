Open Flow Prototype
Primary Action

Each turn the player is given either one or two dice. The player can place them on the board by dragging the dice onto the grid. Before placing, the player can also rotate a pair by tapping on it — each tap rotates it 90°. Once placed, dice are fixed. The player can also "hold" dice using the parking option. Parking can hold one die or pair at a time. Dragging from the spawner to a filled parking spot swaps their contents.

Match Rule

Three or more dice of the same value placed adjacent to each other combine, match, and merge — adding to the score. If the sum of the merging dice exceeds 6, the matching dice disappear from the board. If the sum is ≤ 6, they are replaced by one die showing the sum. For example: three 1-dice → one 3-die.

Board Modifiers
Null Block A permanently blocked cell. You cannot place dice here — it just takes up space. Shown as a muted grey square with a small circular mark.
❄
Frozen Cell A locked cell placed at fixed corner or edge positions. Cannot be played on. Shown as an icy blue cell with a snowflake (❄).
⇆
Flip Die Sits on the board and waits. Place a die next to it and it inverts all its neighbours' values (7 − value), then vanishes. Shown as a plum cell with a double arrow (⇆).
3
Bomb Die A countdown fuse on the board. Every new spawn wave ticks it down by one. When it hits zero, it detonates and you lose. Pulses urgently when the fuse is at 1. Shown as a coral red cell with the remaining fuse count.
☣
Diseased Die Any die placed adjacent to a diseased cell is immediately infected — its value is forced to 6. Cannot be merged away. Shown as a green cell with a biohazard symbol (☣).
★
Wild Die Appears in the spawner tray. Joins any merge group regardless of value, but cannot start a merge on its own. Shown as a blue→plum→coral gradient face with a star (★).
Tutorial (5 steps)
1Place your dice: You get 1 or 2 dice each turn. Drag them on an empty cell to place them on the grid.
2Rotate and match: Rotate your dice by tapping on them once. Place the matching next to each other.
3Merge and add to score: You score points merging three or more similar dice. If the total adds up to 6, the dice disappear from the board.
4Parking spot: Rotate your piece or stash it in the parking spot to play something else first.
5More pairs = higher score: Increase your score by matching all the dice on the grid.

Skill Expression

An expert plans ahead — leaving space to avoid premature merges so they can form bigger, higher-value combinations. A beginner places dice close together to quickly get three of the same type.

Animation Mood

Calm. When dice combine, the animation should feel satisfying — merges have a pop/scale effect and score points animate upward from the merge position.
